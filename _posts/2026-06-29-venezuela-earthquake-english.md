The death toll from the devastating twin earthquakes that struck north-central Venezuela on June 24 has surpassed 1,400, with thousands more injured and nearly 69,000 people reported missing.

## The Quakes

According to the US Geological Survey, a magnitude 7.2 foreshock struck at 6:04 PM local time, followed just 39 seconds later by a magnitude 7.5 mainshock. The epicenters were located near Morón in Carabobo state, approximately 168 km west of Caracas, at depths of 10-22 km.

Venezuela lies outside the Pacific Ring of Fire but sits on the boundary between the Caribbean and South American plates, where infrequent but severe earthquakes can occur.

## Damage and Casualties

As of June 28:
- **Fatalities**: At least 1,450
- **Injured**: Over 3,000
- **Missing**: Approximately 68,900
- **Potentially affected**: Up to 7 million people

Multiple high-rise buildings and residential towers collapsed in Caracas and Valencia. The Simón Bolívar International Airport sustained significant damage, including a partial roof collapse, forcing its closure. Subway and natural gas services in Caracas were suspended.

## International Response

Acting President Delcy Rodríguez declared a state of emergency. The United States, Brazil, Colombia, and other nations have deployed rescue teams and medical supplies. The US State Department mobilized disaster assistance teams and search-and-rescue specialists.

El Salvador's President Nayib Bukele and Ecuador's President Daniel Noboa also pledged humanitarian aid.

## Looking Ahead

The USGS estimates the final death toll could range from 1,000 to 10,000. Local hospitals are overwhelmed, and field hospitals are being set up. World Vision has begun emergency relief operations, with shelter, clean water, and medical care identified as the most urgent needs.

Search teams continue working around the clock, racing to find survivors trapped beneath the rubble before the critical 72-hour window closes.

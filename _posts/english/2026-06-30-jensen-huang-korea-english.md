Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang visited South Korea on June 5, his first trip in seven months. He met with the chairmen of Samsung Electronics, SK Group, Hyundai Motor Group, LG Group, and Naver to discuss AI and robotics collaboration.

## Key Announcements

Huang made two major announcements during his visit. First, Nvidia has begun hiring for a Korea R&D center, with plans to build a dedicated facility once sufficient personnel are secured. He described Korea as "an excellent place to invest in R&D" given its expertise in AI, robotics, and semiconductor technology.

Second, he declared that "robotics is going to be the next major sector here in Korea." He noted that Korea's manufacturing and mechatronics strengths make it "perfectly positioned for the fusion required for robotics."

Huang told reporters he "brought a lot of business for Korea" with "some surprises," though he declined to disclose specifics.

## Business Meetings

The visit included a dinner gathering with SK Group Chairman Chey Tae-won, Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung, LG Chairman Koo Kwang-mo, and Naver Chairman Lee Hae-jin. Huang also met with game companies NCSoft and Krafton.

Discussions covered HBM (high-bandwidth memory) supply, next-generation AI accelerators, physical AI, and smart factory collaboration. SK Hynix, Nvidia's largest HBM supplier, was a key focus.

Huang also confirmed that Nvidia's next-generation Vera Rubin AI accelerator platform will enter mass production in the second half of 2026.

## Market Impact

LG Electronics shares hit the daily upper limit of 29.93%, reaching an all-time high. LG Innotek, LG CNS, and other LG affiliates also surged. Naver and Hyundai Motor shares gained on partnership expectations.

## Strategic Significance

Analysts view Huang's visit as signaling Nvidia's broader bet on Korea's AI ecosystem beyond chip procurement. "This is an opportunity to gauge what role South Korea could play in Nvidia's global AI infrastructure expansion strategy," said Meritz Securities analyst Hwang Soo-wook.

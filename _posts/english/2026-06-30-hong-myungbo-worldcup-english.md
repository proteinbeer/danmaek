South Korea's national football team was eliminated in the group stage of the 2026 FIFA World Cup, finishing 34th overall. Head coach Hong Myung-bo resigned, taking responsibility for the failure.

## Tournament Performance

Drawn in Group A with hosts Mexico, Czech Republic, and South Africa, Korea won their opening match 2-1 against Czech Republic but lost 0-1 to Mexico and 0-1 to South Africa. Among 12 third-placed teams competing for eight remaining spots, Korea ranked 10th.

This marks Korea's first group stage elimination since 2018 and repeats Hong's failure at the 2014 Brazil World Cup, where he also finished with 1 draw and 2 losses.

## Hong's Resignation

Hong announced his resignation on June 29 at the team base camp in Zapopan, Mexico. His contract, running through the 2027 Asian Cup, was cut short by about six months. "All responsibility lies with me," he said. "I am deeply sorry to the people."

## KFA Under Fire

The failure has exposed deep-seated problems within the Korea Football Association (KFA). Hong's appointment in 2024 was controversial from the start, with a government audit revealing that the recruitment process was unfair — Hong was effectively pre-selected after meeting with an official who lacked authority to recommend candidates.

KFA President Chung Mong-kyu, who secured a fourth term despite calls for reform, faces accusations of maintaining a "cartel" structure where alumni networks dominate key positions. President Lee Jae-myung criticized the "incompetent commander selection" and announced sports administration reforms.

## Player Management Questions

Hong's decision to bench captain Son Heung-min for the crucial South Africa match drew sharp criticism. The team's best player was introduced as a substitute in the second half when time was already running out. Star player Lee Kang-in was deployed in defensive roles rather than his natural attacking midfielder position, limiting his creative impact.

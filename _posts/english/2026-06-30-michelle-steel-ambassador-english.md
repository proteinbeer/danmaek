The US Senate confirmed former California Congresswoman Michelle Steel as the new US Ambassador to South Korea on June 17, with a 55-39 vote.

## Who Is Michelle Steel?

Steel was born in Seoul in 1955 to parents who fled North Korea. She was raised in Japan and immigrated to the US at age 19. She earned degrees from Pepperdine University (BA) and the University of Southern California (MBA).

Her political career began with election to the California State Board of Equalization in 2006, followed by the Orange County Board of Supervisors (2015-2021), and two terms in the US House of Representatives (2021-2025). She lost her 2024 re-election bid to Democrat Derek Tran.

Steel made history in 2020 as one of the first Korean American women elected to Congress, alongside Rep. Young Kim and Rep. Marilyn Strickland.

## Second Korean American Ambassador

Steel becomes the second Korean American to serve as US ambassador to South Korea, following Ambassador Sung Kim (2011-2014). She is also the second female US ambassador to Korea after Kathleen Stephens (2008-2011).

The ambassadorial post had been vacant since Ambassador Philip Goldberg left in January 2025, with Chargé d'Affaires James Heller serving as interim.

## Diplomatic Agenda

During her confirmation hearing, Steel pledged to ensure "equal treatment" for American companies in South Korea. She will face several pressing challenges:

- Coordinating US-ROK response to North Korea's advancing nuclear and missile threats
- Managing defense cost-sharing negotiations for US Forces Korea (28,500 troops)
- Navigating semiconductor supply chain cooperation amid US-China tensions
- Addressing South Korea's role in Middle East security, including Strait of Hormuz contributions

South Korea's Foreign Ministry welcomed Steel's confirmation, stating she "will contribute to strengthening bilateral relations and promoting friendship between both countries."

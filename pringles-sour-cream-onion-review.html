Pringles Sour Cream & Onion Review

Everyone knows Pringles, right? The stackable potato chips in a cylindrical tube! Last week, the green can caught my eye at the supermarket, so I picked one up. It's Pringles Sour Cream & Onion. The price was about 3,500 won.

I looked into Pringles' history and found it interesting. It was first created in 1968 by P&G (Procter & Gamble) in the USA. Apparently, it was developed to solve the problems of existing potato chips â€” they break easily, are greasy, and come in bags with lots of air. The unique saddle shape (mathematically called a hyperbolic paraboloid) and the iconic paperboard tube container are the result. It moved to Kellanova in 2012 and then to Mars in 2024.

First Impression

The smell when I opened the lid was really good. The savory aroma of sour cream and the sweet-savory scent of onion blend together in a way you don't often get from Korean chips. It wasn't spicy â€” it was gentle but kept calling me back for more.

Pringles' biggest feature is perfect uniformity. Every single chip is the same shape, the same thickness, and the same size. That's because they're made from a dough of dried potato flakes and starches rather than real sliced potatoes. Thanks to this, each chip is seasoned evenly, giving a consistent taste every time. The sight of them stacked neatly in the tube was also highly satisfying.

Taste and Texture

What hits you on the first bite is the gentle tang of sour cream and the umami of onion. The sourness isn't strong â€” it spreads softly, followed by the savory flavor of onion. It feels clean and less oily on the fingers compared to some traditional potato chips, making it incredibly easy to snack on.

The texture is intensely crispy. It delivers a sharp, clean snap with every bite. They break easily, but that's actually part of the charm. Crushing the ridges with your tongue is strangely addictive. Before you know it, you've eaten ten in one go.

Flavor stages:

  • Start: The gentle tang of sour cream spreads across your palate â€” a refreshing sensation
  • Middle: The savory, slightly sweet taste of onion powder comes through
  • Finish: A mild saltiness lingers, but it's not overwhelming

Who Would Like This?

This is perfect for anyone looking for a savory, light-feeling snack. I'd recommend it to people who find spicy snacks too much, or who think cheese snacks are too heavy. It's also great as a kids' snack since the onion flavor is mild and sweet rather than sharp, so even children enjoy it.

Beer Pairing

Pringles Sour Cream & Onion was a fantastic match with wheat beer (Weissbier). The fruity notes of the wheat beer and the smooth sour cream flavor of Pringles complemented each other perfectly. Among soft drinks, ginger ale worked best. The ginger aroma subtly harmonized with the onion flavor.

Nutritional Info

One serving (30g, about 14-15 chips) contains around 150 kcal, 9g of fat, 16g of carbs, and 190mg of sodium. While the sodium per serving is relatively moderate for a savory snack, the fat content is just as high as regular potato chips, and since you can easily eat half a tube in one sitting, it's best to pace yourself.

Overall Verdict

Pringles Sour Cream & Onion is a product that sets the standard for global potato chips. Consistent quality, a mild flavor, and an addictive texture â€” it hits all three notes. It's not a flashy snack, but when you're bored and want something to munch on, there's nothing quite like it.

Would I buy it again? Yes, I think I'll keep stocking up on these. It's a reliable choice, and the flavor never gets old.

Where to buy: You can easily find them anywhere â€” hypermarkets, convenience stores, Coupang, Gmarket, and more.

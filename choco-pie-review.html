Orion Choco Pie Review: Cake, Marshmallow, and Chocolate

Since Orion launched its Choco Pie in 1974, the individually wrapped cake has become a familiar Korean lunchbox and sharing snack. Its combination of soft cake, marshmallow, and chocolate-style coating also travels well, which helped it find an audience outside Korea.

Choco Pies also appeared in reporting about the Kaesong Industrial Complex, where South Korean companies distributed them to North Korean workers as snacks. Their exchange outside the workplace added an unusual political chapter to the product's history, although accounts and valuations varied.

Orion launched its Choco Pie in 1974, adapting the familiar combination of cake, marshmallow, and a chocolate coating into an individually wrapped snack. The format traveled well and became closely associated with the Orion name in Korea and overseas.

What's Inside?

The snack consists of two soft, spongy chocolate-flavored cake rounds with a fluffy marshmallow center, all enrobed in a thin, crisp chocolate coating. The texture is the real star here — the cake is intentionally designed to absorb moisture from the marshmallow layer over time, making it tender and crumbly. The marshmallow gives a gentle, satisfying chew without being overly sticky or dense.

At about 130 calories per pie, it's a perfectly portioned sweet treat. The original version has been a beloved icon for over 50 years with its core formula remaining carefully guarded — a true testament to the philosophy of "if it ain't broke, don't fix it."

The eating experience breaks down like this:

  • First bite: The thin chocolate shell snaps cleanly, revealing the soft, crumbly baked cake underneath.
  • The chew: The marshmallow center stretches slightly as you bite through, pillowy and soft.
  • The finish: The cake and marshmallow meld together seamlessly, leaving a comforting, balanced chocolate sweetness.

Flavors Beyond the Original

Orion has released several incredibly popular limited edition and permanent flavor variations over the years:

  • Banana Choco Pie: Infused with real banana puree inside the marshmallow center, creating a rich banana milk aroma that became a massive hit across Asia.
  • Strawberry Choco Pie: Featuring berry-infused fillings or pink coatings, often launched as a spring seasonal special.
  • Matcha / Green Tea Choco Pie: Utilizing deep green tea-infused sponge cakes paired with the sweet white chocolate or classic glaze for a more mature profile.

The global footprint of this snack is staggering. In Russia, Choco Pie is an absolute household staple, with Orion operating multiple local factories to supply hundreds of millions of pies annually. Similarly, Vietnam consumes immense quantities each year, where the snack is even utilized as a traditional offering on family altars due to its premium reputation.

Nutrition & Value

One individual Choco Pie (28g) contains about 130 calories, 5g of fat (2.5g saturated), 19g of carbohydrates (11g sugar), and 2g of protein. A standard box of 12 is highly affordable, usually found at H Mart or local Asian grocers for around $5–$6, making it an incredibly economical comfort treat.

Verdict

Orion Choco Pie keeps its formula simple: soft cake, a chewy marshmallow layer, and a thin chocolate coating. The portions are tidy and the contrasting textures are the main attraction.

The word "Jeong" (情) on the packaging refers to affection and social bonds, a theme Orion has used in its marketing for decades. That emphasis on sharing helps explain the snack's nostalgic reputation. Warming one briefly can soften the marshmallow and coating, but remove the wrapper first and use only a few seconds at a time.

Best for: Anyone looking for a portable cake snack with a soft center and moderate chocolate flavor.

Where to buy: H Mart, local Korean grocery stores, Coupang, Gmarket, and Amazon.

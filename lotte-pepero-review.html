Pepero: Korea's Favorite Chocolate Snack Stick

I picked up a box of Lotte Pepero at H Mart last week — the Original Chocolate flavor — for $2.49. I'd seen these thin cookie sticks all over Korean dramas and convenience store vlogs, and November 11th (Pepero Day!) is a whole thing in Korea where people exchange these like Valentine's Day candy. So I had to find out what makes these little sticks so special.

Pepero has been around since 1983, made by Lotte Wellfood. The name comes from the Korean descriptive word "Pae-pae" (빼빼), which refers to something long, thin, and slender — fitting, since these are basically slender biscuit sticks dipped in sweet chocolate. They're exported to about 64 countries now, heavily driven by the massive Pepero Day tradition (11/11, because the standing sticks look like the number 1).

First Impressions

The packaging is straightforward — a thin cardboard box containing a single sealed foil pouch that holds the sticks safely together. The sticks themselves are about as thick as a pencil, maybe slightly thinner. The chocolate coating looks smooth and evenly applied, leaving the bottom inch of the biscuit bare so you can hold it without getting chocolate on your fingers.

Opening the pouch, the aroma is pure, comforting milk chocolate — simple, sweet, and familiar, with a nice toasted biscuit undertone.

Taste and Texture

First bite: a highly satisfying crunch. The biscuit inside is light and crisp — way more delicate and airier than a standard pretzel stick. It snaps cleanly and crumbles perfectly as you chew. The chocolate coating is smooth and sweet, providing a great balance without being overly rich.

The balance is really well executed. The biscuit-to-chocolate ratio is close to 50:50, so it doesn't feel like you're just eating a heavy candy bar. The baked biscuit provides an excellent structural crunch and a very subtle saltiness that keeps the sweetness in check. It's an incredibly easy snack to mindlessly munch on.

I also grabbed the Almond version. The crushed almond bits embedded in the glaze add a fantastic textural contrast — giving an extra layer of nutty crunchiness on top of the crisp biscuit. The chocolate coating is slightly thicker on the Almond version too. Between the two, the Almond is probably my favorite for the extra depth, though the Original wins on classic elegance.

Flavor breakdown (Original):

  • First: Sweet milk chocolate hits your tongue — creamy, smooth, and comforting
  • Middle: The biscuit crunch kicks in — light, crisp, with a subtle malty flavor
  • Finish: A clean aftertaste where the slight saltiness of the baked biscuit neatly cuts the sugar

How It Compares to Pocky

You can't talk about Pepero without mentioning Pocky — the Japanese version by Ezaki Glico. While they look nearly identical, the differences are subtle. Pepero's chocolate coating tends to be slightly thicker and creamier, while Pocky features a thinner, snappier chocolate layer. The biscuit stick in Pepero is also a tiny bit wider and more robust. (Fun fact: Glico actually filed a design patent lawsuit against Lotte in 2014, but it was specifically over the geometric packaging design of Lotte's premium "Pepero Premier" line, not the classic sticks themselves). Both are great, but Pepero carries immense cultural weight if you love Korean food.

Pepero Day Culture

November 11th is Pepero Day in South Korea, and it's a massive cultural phenomenon. People give boxes of Pepero to friends, crushes, family, and coworkers — similar to Valentine's Day but with way less pressure. Lotte reportedly sees a monumental spike in annual sales around this single day. They also frequently run limited-edition collaborations with top K-pop groups like Stray Kids and NewJeans, creating collectible boxes that fans snap up instantly. It's wild how a simple snack stick turned into a major annual event.

Nutrition

Per box (47g, which yields about 20 sticks): Around 240 kcal, 10g fat, 35g carbs (18g sugar), and 3g protein. While the full box packs a fair amount of sugar, the light format makes it a great companion for an afternoon pick-me-up. Paired with a hot cup of black coffee, a few sticks make for the perfect treats.

Verdict

Pepero is simplicity done right. It's not trying to be an artisanal, gourmet chocolate bar — it's a fun, crunchy, highly shareable snack that has been perfected over four decades. The thin stick format keeps it feeling light while completely satisfying your sweet tooth.

Would I buy again? Definitely. It's a fantastic staple to keep in the pantry, and a box is perfect for throwing into a bag for a quick, mess-free sweet craving on the go.

Where to buy: H Mart, Amazon, Korean grocery stores, and the Asian food section of most major supermarkets.

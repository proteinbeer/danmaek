Lotte Kancho Review: Crisp Biscuit with Chocolate Filling

There are some snacks that define childhood. In Korea, Lotte Kancho is one of them. These small, round, bite-sized biscuits packed with a sweet chocolate cream filling have been a lunchbox staple for generations. First launched in 1984, Kancho has been satisfying sweet cravings for over 40 years.

Kancho is usually sold in a small yellow box with a sealed foil bag inside. The round biscuits carry assorted cartoon prints, giving the otherwise simple package a playful, retro look. Prices vary by store and country, so imported boxes can cost considerably more than they do in Korea.

What makes Kancho special is its clean yet satisfying structure. Unlike messy chocolate-coated bars or delicate wafers, it seals a rich, smooth chocolate cream inside a crisp, hollow biscuit shell, making it the perfect mess-free sweet treat.

First Impression

Opening the box, the aroma is unmistakable — sweet, baked biscuit dough with a rich undertone of milk chocolate. The biscuits are loose in the bag, featuring a variety of round, golden-tan spheres with the signature Kancho characters stamped directly onto the top of each piece. The random character faces printed on the biscuits add a fun, playful element to the experience.

Each piece is a small round biscuit, roughly marble-sized. The smooth outer shell gives way quickly to a light, airy crunch, followed by the softer chocolate filling.

The first bite confirms the delightful balance. The crisp biscuit shell shatters cleanly between your teeth, instantly releasing the smooth, sweet chocolate cream hiding inside. It's a classic flavor combination executed with perfect contrast.

Taste and Texture

The flavor profile is straightforward but incredibly well-executed. The outer biscuit is mildly sweet and buttery, providing a clean structural base. The chocolate cream filling is the standout â€" it has a smooth, milky, and creamy consistency that melts easily.

Texture-wise, Kancho delivers a satisfying progression: the sharp snap of the outer biscuit shell, followed immediately by the smooth richness of the chocolate center. It's noticeably less sweet and heavy compared to many Western chocolate snacks, showcasing the balanced restraint that Korean confectionery is famous for.

The pieces are small and easy to portion, although the bite-sized format also makes it easy to lose track of a serving.

Flavor stages:

  • Start: A light, buttery biscuit crunch with a clean snap
  • Middle: The shell gives way to a burst of smooth, milky chocolate cream
  • Finish: A comforting, mild chocolate sweetness that lingers just enough

Who Would Like This?

Kancho is an absolute crowd-pleaser. Kids love the fun round shapes and the playful character faces printed on each piece. Adults appreciate the neat, bite-sized portions and the deep nostalgia factor. If you enjoy chocolate-filled cookies like Koala's March or Meiji Hello Panda, Kancho is right up your alley.

It's a great snack for lunchboxes, desk drawers, or a quick afternoon pick-me-up. The neat design means no melted chocolate on your fingers, making it easy to share or enjoy on the go. It's also a common sight at Korean children's parties and school events.

Pairing-wise, Kancho goes surprisingly well with coffee. The mild milk chocolate and biscuit flavors complement a black Americano or a hot latte beautifully. For a more traditional, comforting treat, try it with a cold glass of milk.

Nutritional Info

Per serving (54g full box): Around 260 kcal, 11g fat (6g saturated), 37g carbohydrates, 16g sugar, 160mg sodium, and 4g protein. The portion size is perfect for a quick sweet craving without going overboard.

Overall Verdict

Lotte Kancho is a classic for good reason. It doesn't try to be overly fancy or complicated — it just delivers a reliable, highly satisfying chocolate biscuit experience that's been perfected over four decades. The hidden chocolate center keeps things clean, and the fun character prints make every bite a little surprise.

Best for: Fans of crisp, chocolate-filled biscuits who want a neat snack for lunchboxes, desks, or travel.

Where to buy: Any Korean convenience store (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven), H Mart, Coupang, Gmarket, Amazon, and most Asian grocery stores.

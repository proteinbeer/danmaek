Nongshim Onion Ring Review

There are certain Korean snacks that have been around so long they feel like part of the culture. Nongshim Onion Ring is one of them. First released in 1983, this ring-shaped snack has been a staple in Korean convenience stores and households for over 40 years.

I picked up a bag at the local GS25 for about 1,500 won. The bright yellow and orange packaging immediately caught my eye — it has that retro vibe that feels nostalgic even if you've never tried it before.

What sets Onion Ring apart from other snacks is its unique ring shape and distinct onion flavor. It's made from wheat flour and real onion powder, giving it both a satisfying crunch and a savory kick that you don't get from standard potato chips.

First Impression

The first thing I noticed was the aroma. As soon as I opened the bag, a wave of sweet, savory onion scent hit me. It's not artificial or harsh — it smells like real cooked onion. The rings themselves are uniform in size, perfectly round with a hole in the middle, and coated in a light dusting of seasoning.

Grabbing one, the texture felt light and airy. It's not as dense as a potato chip — more like a puffed snack with a satisfying crunch that doesn't feel heavy. The ring shape makes it fun to eat, and honestly, the design is part of the appeal.

Taste and Texture

The first bite delivers an immediate burst of onion flavor — savory, slightly sweet, with a hint of bacon-like smokiness. The seasoning is well-balanced: not too salty, not too mild. It's savory without being overwhelming, and the onion taste feels natural rather than chemical.

The texture is where this snack really shines. It's crunchy but light — almost airy. The ring shape gives it a unique mouthfeel that changes as you bite through it. At first it's crisp, then it dissolves quickly on the tongue, leaving the onion flavor to linger.

Unlike many other snacks that coat your fingers in powder, Onion Ring leaves surprisingly little mess. The seasoning sticks to the ring well, which means less orange fingers later.

Flavor stages:

  • Start: A rush of sweet onion with a savory undertone
  • Middle: The smokiness kicks in, with a hint of bacon and umami
  • Finish: A clean, savory aftertaste that makes you reach for another

Who Would Like This?

This is a great snack for anyone who enjoys savory treats. It's not spicy at all, so kids and adults alike can enjoy it. The onion flavor is prominent but not overwhelming, making it a good choice for people who find cheese snacks too rich or spicy snacks too intense.

It's also a fantastic beer snack. The light, crunchy texture and savory onion flavor pair exceptionally well with lager or pale ale. Among soft drinks, cola works nicely — the sweetness contrasts with the savory onion.

Nutritional Info

Per 80g serving: 375kcal, 16g fat, 58g carbohydrates, 5g sugar, 550mg sodium, 2.8g protein. It's relatively moderate for a snack food, though the sodium is noticeable if you eat the whole bag in one sitting — which is very easy to do.

Overall Verdict

Nongshim Onion Ring is a timeless Korean snack for good reason. It delivers consistent quality, a unique texture, and a flavor that's both simple and addictive. It's not trying to be trendy or over-the-top — it just does one thing well and has been doing it for 40 years.

It won't blow your mind, but it doesn't need to. It's a reliable, everyday snack that hits the spot every time.

Would I buy it again? Absolutely. It's going into my regular snack rotation alongside Saewookkang and Honey Butter Chip.

Where to buy: Any Korean convenience store (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven), Coupang, Gmarket, H Mart, and Amazon.

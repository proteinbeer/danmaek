I Finally Tried the Legendary Honey Butter Chip

So I finally picked up a bag of Haitai Honey Butter Chip at H Mart last week. $4.99 for a 120g bag — not cheap, but I've been hearing about this thing forever. Like, the legendary "Honey Butter Craze" of 2014? People were literally fighting over these in convenience stores, reselling them at 50x the price online, and even Girls' Generation's Sooyoung posted selfies with it. A whole decade later, I had to see what the fuss was about.

For context: this chip came out in August 2014, made by Haitai-Calbee (that's a joint venture between Korean Haitai and Japanese Calbee). It sold so fast stores had "sold out" signs up for months. At its peak, a single 60g bag was going for 50,000 won — like $50 USD — on Korean e-commerce sites. Fifty bucks for a bag of chips. Crazy, right?

First Impressions

The bag is really simple honestly. Golden yellow with a dripping honey illustration, nothing flashy. But when I opened it — whoa. The first thing that hits you is butter. Not that fake artificial butter flavor, but something way closer to real pastry butter, like the smell you get walking into a bakery. Turns out Haitai uses French gourmet butter that's apparently fermented for over 12 hours. Fancy!

The chips themselves are thin — slightly thinner than Lay's — with a light golden color and visible seasoning dust. They look cleaner somehow, less oily than most American chips I've had.

Taste and Texture

First bite: crisp. Clean snap, not greasy at all. The seasoning hits you right away — sweet honey up front, then this wave of buttery richness, and finally a subtle saltiness at the back that balances everything out.

The best part? It's not overwhelmingly sweet. That's the secret I think. The honey is gentle, like someone lightly brushed it on. The butter flavor is warm and creamy, almost like a butter cookie more than a potato chip. And the salt pulls it back just before it gets too much.

I finished half the bag without even realizing it. Like, I looked down and half was just gone. That's both a compliment and a warning — the 120g bag has about 690 kcal total, so uh, portion control is hard here.

How It Compares

I've had Lay's Honey Butter before (they have it in their global flavors line). The Haitai version is noticeably better — the butter tastes real, the sweetness is more refined, and the chip itself is crispier and less oily. Lay's leans sweeter with a more artificial butter aftertaste. There's also Nongshim's Sumi Chip Honey Mustard, which came out as a competitor during the craze — more mustard-forward, less sweet, but easier to find these days.

At $4.99 it's definitely pricier than a standard bag of Lay's ($3-4). But hey, it's an import and you're paying for the real deal.

Nutrition

Per 60g serving (half the bag): 345 kcal, 22g fat (around 7g saturated), 34g carbohydrates, 4g sugar, and about 210mg of sodium. While the sodium is lower than your average savory potato chip, the fat content is relatively high due to the real butter blend, so it's a rich treat best enjoyed in moderation.

Verdict

Okay so, Honey Butter Chip actually deserves the hype. It's not just a gimmick. The sweet-salty-buttery balance is genuinely addictive, the texture is perfect, and I totally get why this became a whole cultural thing in Korea. The French butter really does make a difference — you can taste it.

Would I buy again? Yeah, for sure. It's my go-to Korean snack now when I want something different from regular potato chips. Just wish it were a little cheaper though.

Where to buy: H Mart, Amazon, Korean grocery stores

Doritos Nacho Cheese Review: Straight-Up American Nostalgia

A few days ago I stopped by the neighborhood mart and spotted Doritos Nacho Cheese in the imported snack section. The very snack I'd only ever seen on American YouTube and movies! I thought, "Oh, so this is the famous Doritos," and grabbed a bag. It was about 2,500 won. They go for about 3-4 dollars in the US, so roughly the same.

Doritos first came out in 1964. I found it interesting that the brand started when a restaurant in Disneyland used leftover tortillas. Now it's a global snack sold worldwide. The Nacho Cheese flavor launched in 1972 and has been a bestseller in America ever since.

First Impressions

The bag uses a red-and-orange design that really grabs your attention. The colors are intense and instantly eye-catching. The smell when I opened it was truly unique — not something you'd typically find in Korean snacks. The cheese aroma comes through quite strong, but rather than the pungent scent of processed cheese, it's a subtle, rich fragrance.

Doritos are definitely different from Korean snacks. Made from masa flour tortilla chips, they're triangular and have a substantial thickness. They're firmer than Korean potato chips, and you can taste the savory corn flavor as you chew.

Taste and Texture

As for taste: the first thing you notice is rich cheese flavor. Unlike Korean cheese snacks, which tend to be mild, this one fills your mouth with a pretty intense cheese taste. They say it's a blend of three cheeses — cheddar, Monterey Jack, and one more — and when you eat it, you get a complex flavor with a slight tang, not just plain cheese. The umami from tomato and onion comes through subtly, blending with the cheese in an impressive way.

The texture is pure crunch. To exaggerate a bit, there's a satisfying pleasure in biting through it. Among Korean snacks, Nongshim Sumi Chip is the closest comparison, but Doritos are firmer and need more chewing, which makes the savory flavor last longer.

Breaking down the taste by stages:

  • First: The savory cheese flavor fills your mouth — it's quite rich
  • Middle: A subtle spicy aftertaste comes through — not hot, more like a gentle chili powder spice
  • Finish: A salty, umami-tinged aftertaste lingers — the kind of magic that keeps your hand reaching for more

It might be a bit salty for Korean palates. The sodium content is definitely higher than Korean snacks. But that's part of the appeal. It's the perfect accompaniment to drinks.

Who Would I Recommend It To?

If you love cheese snacks, you have to try this. There's a reason people call it the essence of American snacks. I especially recommend it to anyone looking for a beer snack. Kids will enjoy the taste too, but since it's quite salty, it's best to moderate their intake.

Beer Pairing

My personal favorite combination was crisp lager + Doritos Nacho Cheese. The cold beer washes away the saltiness while the cheese flavor pairs wonderfully with the beer's bitterness. Among soft drinks, I preferred cider over cola. The sweetness of cider seems to harmonize with the saltiness of the cheese.

Nutritional Info

About 140 kcal per serving (28g). It has around 170mg of sodium and 1.5g of saturated fat. It's a snack, so some indulgence is inevitable, but eating too much at once does raise concerns about sodium intake. Best to enjoy in moderation.

Overall Verdict

Doritos Nacho Cheese really shows you the power of a classic. There's a reason this flavor has been loved for over 50 years. The cheese flavor is on a completely different level from Korean cheese snacks. The price is a bit higher than Korean snacks, but it has a charm that makes you crave it every now and then.

Would I buy again: Yes, I think I'll crave it from time to time. Especially as the perfect snack with beer.

Where to buy: Available at the imported snack section of large marts, Coupang, GMarket, Emart, etc.

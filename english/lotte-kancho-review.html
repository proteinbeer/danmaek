Lotte Kancho Review

There are some snacks that define childhood. In Korea, Lotte Kancho is one of them. These thin, crispy wafer sticks filled with peanut cream and coated in chocolate have been a lunchbox staple for generations. First launched in 1975, Kancho has been satisfying sweet cravings for nearly 50 years.

I picked up a box at a local convenience store for about 1,200 won. The packaging is simple and retro — a bright yellow box with the iconic Kancho logo and a cartoon illustration of what looks like a cheerful wafer character. Inside, each stick is individually wrapped, making it great for portion control or sharing.

What makes Kancho special is the combination of textures. Unlike solid chocolate bars or plain wafer snacks, Kancho layers crunchy wafer, smooth peanut cream, and a thin chocolate coating into one bite-sized stick. It's a simple concept that's been perfected over decades.

First Impression

Opening the box, the aroma is unmistakable — sweet chocolate with a nutty peanut undertone. The sticks are neatly arranged in rows, each one wrapped in foil with the signature blue and yellow Kancho design. The individual wrapping is practical and adds to the nostalgic feel.

I unwrapped one and was struck by its size. It's a thin, delicate stick, about the length of a finger but much slimmer. The chocolate coating has a slight sheen, and you can see the wafer layers peeking through at the ends. It feels light in the hand, almost fragile.

The first bite confirms what the smell promised. The chocolate coating cracks open, giving way to a crispy wafer that shatters between your teeth. Then the peanut cream hits — smooth, nutty, and just sweet enough to balance the chocolate. It's a flavor journey packed into a single stick.

Taste and Texture

The flavor profile is straightforward but well-executed. The chocolate coating is mild and milky, similar to a Hershey's bar but less waxy. The peanut cream filling is the standout — it has a genuine roasted peanut flavor with a creamy, almost fudge-like consistency. The wafer itself is light and crispy, providing the perfect structural base.

Texture-wise, Kancho delivers a satisfying progression: snap of the chocolate shell, crunch of the wafer, then the smooth richness of the peanut cream. It's not overly sweet compared to Western chocolate snacks. Korean snacks tend to be more restrained with sugar, and Kancho is a good example of that balance.

Each stick is about 54 calories, making it easy to eat a few without guilt. The individual wrapping also keeps them fresh longer than a opened bag of chips would.

Flavor stages:

  • Start: Milky chocolate sweetness with a crisp snap
  • Middle: Crunchy wafer gives way to smooth peanut cream
  • Finish: Nutty, slightly sweet aftertaste that lingers just enough

Who Would Like This?

Kancho is a crowd-pleaser. Kids love it for the fun stick format and sweet chocolate coating. Adults appreciate the portion control and the nostalgic factor. If you enjoy peanut butter and chocolate combinations like Reese's cups, you'll likely enjoy Kancho's lighter, crispier take on the same idea.

It's a great snack for lunchboxes, desk drawers, or as a quick afternoon pick-me-up. The individual wrapping makes it easy to toss in a bag without worrying about crumbs or melting. It's also a common sight at Korean children's birthday parties and school events.

Pairing-wise, Kancho goes surprisingly well with coffee. The mild chocolate and peanut flavors complement a black Americano or a latte. For a more indulgent treat, try it with a glass of cold milk — the classic combination.

Nutritional Info

Per 2-stick serving (28g): 140kcal, 8g fat, 16g carbohydrates, 9g sugar, 40mg sodium, 2g protein. The sugar content is moderate for a chocolate snack, and the individually wrapped sticks make it easier to stick to one serving compared to a bag of chips.

Overall Verdict

Lotte Kancho is a classic for good reason. It doesn't try to be fancy or innovative — it just delivers a reliable, satisfying chocolate wafer experience that's been perfected over nearly 50 years. The peanut cream filling sets it apart from ordinary wafer bars, and the individual wrapping makes it practical for everyday snacking.

Would I buy it again? Absolutely. It's a great pantry staple for when you want something sweet but not overwhelming. The individually wrapped sticks also make it ideal for packing in a lunch or bringing to work.

Where to buy: Any Korean convenience store (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven), H Mart, Coupang, Gmarket, Amazon, and most Asian grocery stores.

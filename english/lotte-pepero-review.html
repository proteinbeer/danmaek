Pepero: Korea's Favorite Chocolate Snack Stick

I picked up a box of Lotte Pepero at H Mart last week — the Original Chocolate flavor — for $2.49. I'd seen these thin cookie sticks all over Korean dramas and convenience store vlogs, and November 11th (Pepero Day!) is a whole thing in Korea where people exchange these like Valentine's Day candy. So I had to find out what makes these little sticks so special.

Pepero has been around since 1983, made by Lotte Wellfood. The name comes from the Korean word 빼빼로, which apparently refers to something long and thin — fitting, since these are basically slender biscuit sticks dipped in compound chocolate. They're exported to about 64 countries now, and there's even a Pepero Day tradition (11/11, because the sticks look like the number 1).

First Impressions

The packaging is simple — a thin cardboard box with a plastic tray inside holding the sticks in two rows. The sticks are about as thick as a pencil, maybe slightly thinner. The chocolate coating looked smooth and evenly applied. No chips or cracks — the packaging does a good job protecting them.

Opening the box, the smell is just... chocolate. Simple, sweet, milk chocolate. Nothing artificial or overpowering. Each stick is individually wrapped in clear plastic inside the box, which keeps them fresh but feels a bit wasteful honestly.

Taste and Texture

First bite: satisfying crunch. The biscuit inside is light and crisp — way more delicate than a pretzel stick or breadstick. It snaps cleanly, then sort of dissolves as you chew. The chocolate coating is smooth and sweet, but not overly rich.

The balance is really well done. The biscuit-to-chocolate ratio is about 50:50, so it doesn't feel like you're just eating a chocolate bar. The biscuit provides structure and a slight saltiness that keeps the sweetness in check. It's a snack you can eat 5-6 of without feeling overwhelmed by sugar.

I also grabbed the Almond version. The almond bits add a nice textural contrast — extra crunchiness on top of the crisp biscuit. The chocolate coating is slightly thicker on the Almond version too. Between the two, the Almond is probably my favorite, though the Original is more elegant in its simplicity.

Flavor breakdown (Original):

  • First: Sweet milk chocolate hits your tongue — creamy, smooth, familiar
  • Middle: The biscuit crunch kicks in — light, almost airy, with a subtle malty flavor
  • Finish: Clean and not too sweet — the slight saltiness of the biscuit lingers

How It Compares to Pocky

You can't talk about Pepero without mentioning Pocky — the Japanese version by Ezaki Glico. They're basically the same product (Lotte actually got sued by Glico for copying the design in 2014 and lost). The differences are subtle: Pepero's chocolate coating is slightly thicker and creamier, while Pocky has a thinner, snappier chocolate layer. The biscuit stick in Pepero is also a tiny bit wider. Both are great, honestly — but Pepero has that nostalgia factor if you grew up with Korean snacks.

Pepero Day Culture

November 11th is Pepero Day in South Korea, and it's a huge deal. People give boxes of Pepero to friends, crushes, and coworkers — similar to Valentine's Day but way less pressure. Lotte reportedly sees 76% sales increase around Pepero Day. They also do limited edition collaborations with K-pop groups like Stray Kids and TXT, which sell out instantly. It's wild how a simple snack stick turned into a cultural event.

Nutrition

Per box (47g, about 6-7 sticks): 240kcal, 10g fat, 35g carbs (18g sugar), 3g protein. The sugar is a bit high per stick, but since they're individually wrapped, it's easy to portion control. One or two sticks with coffee is the perfect afternoon treat.

Verdict

Pepero is simple done right. It's not trying to be a gourmet chocolate or an artisanal snack — it's a fun, crunchy, chocolatey pick-me-up that's been perfected over 40 years. The thin biscuit stick format makes it feel less heavy than a candy bar while still satisfying that sweet craving.

Would I buy again? Definitely. Great snack to keep in the pantry, and the individually wrapped sticks are perfect for bringing to work or packing in a lunchbox.

Where to buy: H Mart, Amazon, Korean grocery stores, some Asian sections of regular supermarkets

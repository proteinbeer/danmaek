Saewookkang Review: Korea's Iconic Shrimp Snack After All These Years

If you've ever been to a Korean convenience store or browsed a Korean snack aisle, you've almost certainly seen Saewookkang. First released by Nongshim in 1971, this shrimp-flavored cracker has been a staple in Korean households for over five decades. It's the kind of snack you grow up with — eaten as an after-school treat, a late-night beer accompaniment, or just something to mindlessly munch on while watching TV.

The name comes from "saeu" (shrimp) and "kkang" — the latter inspired by the founder's young daughter mispronouncing a folk song. Each 90g bag contains about 4-5 whole shrimp worth of actual shrimp content. It's made using a parching method rather than deep frying, which gives it a distinctive texture and makes it less greasy than typical potato chips.

First Impressions

The unmistakable aroma of roasted shrimp hits you as soon as you tear open the bag. It smells natural and savory — not like artificial shrimp flavoring but the real deal. The crackers themselves are long, rectangular rod shapes with a slightly rough surface that holds the seasoning well. The color is a pale golden beige.

There's a famous Korean recipe where people crush the bag and mix the crumbs with rice — I've never tried it myself, but it speaks to how versatile and beloved this snack is.

Taste and Texture

The first bite gives you a satisfying crunch — firmer and denser than potato chips, with a good amount of resistance before it breaks. The parching method makes it feel less oily, and the savory flavor lingers pleasantly.

The taste is built around umami from real shrimp. It's savory with a subtle sweetness and just the right amount of salt. There's no heavy seasoning masking the base flavor — just clean, straightforward shrimp goodness. The famous Korean jingle says "hands keep reaching for Saewookkang," and it's not wrong. There's something about it that makes you want to keep eating.

Breaking down the experience:

  • First bite: A firm, clean crunch with an immediate burst of shrimp flavor
  • As you chew: The savory umami spreads across your palate — natural and never artificial-tasting
  • Aftertaste: A mild salty-savory finish that makes you reach for another piece

What I appreciate most is that it never gets tiring. Heavily seasoned snacks tend to wear out their welcome after a handful, but Saewookkang's balanced flavor profile keeps you coming back. I think that's why it's survived for 50+ years while trendier snacks have come and gone.

Varieties

Nongshim has expanded the line over the years with several variants:

  • Hot & Spicy Saewookkang (2000): Adds a serious kick with gochugaru seasoning. Quite spicy for a cracker.
  • Rice Saewookkang (2004): Made with rice flour instead of wheat. Lighter texture, less oily.
  • Saewookkang Black (2021): A 50th anniversary edition that became permanent due to popularity. Double the shrimp content with truffle notes. More premium, pricier.
  • Wasabi Saewookkang (2025): A pungent wasabi twist that pairs surprisingly well with the shrimp base.

I've tried the Black version and the truffle aroma is quite pronounced. It's good, but the premium price makes it more of an occasional treat than an everyday snack.

Pairing Suggestions

Saewookkang pairs beautifully with beer — the classic Korean anju (snack with alcohol) combination. The savory shrimp flavor and crispy texture complement a cold lager perfectly. Among soft drinks, I prefer it with cider (Sprite/7Up) over cola — the citrus cleanses the palate between bites.

Surprisingly, it also works well with milk. The saltiness contrasts nicely with milk's creaminess, making it a solid kids' snack combo.

Nutrition Facts

Per 90g bag: 435 kcal, 6g protein, 23g fat, 56g carbs. Calorie count is on the higher side, so sharing is recommended rather than finishing the whole bag in one sitting.

Final Verdict

Saewookkang has earned its place as a Korean snacking institution. It's not flashy or trendy, but it doesn't need to be. The balanced flavor, unique texture, and staying power make it a snack worth appreciating even — or especially — in an era of extreme-flavor novelty snacks.

If you haven't had Saewookkang in a while, pick up a bag. It'll take you back while reminding you why it's stuck around for so long.

Would I buy again? Absolutely. There's always a bag in the pantry.

Where to buy: Korean grocery stores, H Mart, Amazon, Coupang.

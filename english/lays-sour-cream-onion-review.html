Lay's Sour Cream & Onion Review: The American Potato Chip Classic

Whenever you walk into an American convenience store, Lay's is always there. Among the core flavors — BBQ, Original Salted, and Sour Cream & Onion — the last one has always been my favorite. I ordered a bag through Coupang's direct import service. It cost about 2,500 won — cheap enough for a casual snack.

Korea has its own versions — Nongshim Sumi-chip Sour Cream flavor, for example — but the original is Lay's. It's been a steady seller in the US since the 1960s. Lay's itself was founded in 1938 by Herman Lay in Nashville, Tennessee. In 1965, it merged with Frito to become Frito-Lay, which is now part of PepsiCo.

Around the world, Lay's goes by different names. In the UK and Europe it's Walkers, in Australia it's Smith's, and in Mexico it's Sabritas. The taste and quality are nearly identical across the board.

First Impression

The moment I tore open the bag, the rich aroma of sour cream and the sharp kick of onion hit me. The onion scent is surprisingly strong — it immediately felt authentic. The chips themselves are thin and evenly fried to a golden brown, generously coated in seasoning. There was even some loose powder at the bottom of the bag.

The chip thickness is similar to Korean potato chips — thinner than Nongshim Potato Chip, and slightly thinner than Sumi-chip. You can clearly see the oil-fried finish, with a uniform golden color across every chip.

Taste and Texture

First bite: the smooth, creamy flavor of sour cream comes through right away. It spreads across your mouth with a slightly tangy creaminess, followed by the savory, salty kick of onion. It's not a bitter onion taste — it's slightly sweet with deep umami.

Breaking down the flavor:

  • Initial: Smooth and creamy sour cream — a sweet-and-tangy sensation fills your mouth
  • Middle: Onion umami and saltiness kick in — adds depth and richness
  • Finish: A subtle garlic note lingers along with the savory creaminess of sour cream

The texture is classic Lay's. Thin and crispy — one bite and it shatters. The oil level is just right, so your hands don't get too greasy. That said, the chips are thin and break easily, so handle with care.

The best thing about this flavor is that it never gets boring. It's not overly pungent or aggressive, making it a great beer snack or just something to munch on casually. Even finishing a whole bag, it doesn't feel greasy or heavy.

Beer Pairing

Light lagers and pilsners go best with these chips. The crispness of the beer cuts through the creamy sour cream, while the onion flavor complements the beer's bitterness. White wine — especially an aromatic Sauvignon Blanc — works surprisingly well too.

Nutrition Info

One serving (28g) is about 150 kcal, with 170mg of sodium and 1.5g of saturated fat. Pretty standard for potato chips. Half a bag is usually enough to feel satisfied.

Overall Verdict

Lay's Sour Cream & Onion is the classic potato chip done right. It's not flashy, but you'd miss it if it weren't around. You can easily buy it online in Korea now, so I'd recommend giving it a try — especially if you prefer a cleaner, more balanced sour cream flavor compared to local alternatives.

Would I buy it again? Yes. I'll probably order it whenever a craving hits. Coupang Rocket Direct Import delivers fast and reliably.
